Heian Shodan | Pinan Nidan

Heian 1 is composed of basic blocking techniques--downward block, upper level rising block, middle level block with the back of the sword hand--and the middle level straight punch. The stance are the front stance and the back stance. Also included isthe method of countering when your wrist is grasped by a strong opponent. The most important things to master in this kata are reversing direction and leg movements.

Heian Nidan | Pinan Shodan

Techniques practiced in Heian 2 are the upper level block to the side with the upperside of the forearm, front kick and back-fist. For this simultaneous attack to be strong and effective, the *kamae* in movemement 7 must be perfect. It is also important to perfectly master reversing directions and taking the reverse half-front-facing position starting from the same position.

Heian Sandan | Pinan Sandan

Heian 3 consists of changing blcoks--from middle level to lower level--elbow block, back-fist strike, stamping kick and other techniques. Especially important are training in the straddle-leg stance and mastering the unique *tae-sabaki* of sliding the feet (*yori-ashi*).

Heian Yondan | Pinan Yondan

The techniques in Heian 4 are the downward X block and the augemented forearm block, roundhousesword handstrike, vertical elbow strike, reverse wedge block and knee strike. The difficult crossed-feet stance should be mastered. MOvements 1 and 2 are done slowly, but ahnds and feet must move in unison.

Heian Godan | Pinan Godan

The techniques in Heian 5 include the flowing water position, upper level X block, middle level pressing block with both palms, downward block to the side from the straddle-leg stance, middle level hooking block to the side, crescent kick and corssed-feet stance after jumping. In going from the middle level reverse punch from the back stance to the informal attention stance-flowing water position (movements 2-3 and 5-6), head, arms and leg must move in coordination with the hip rotaion. When this is mastered, movements 10, 11 and 12 can be executed continuously. In movement 19, the crossed-feet stance after jumping must be strong. This stance occurs often in the kata.

Tekki Shodan | Naihanchi Shodan

The performance line in the Tekki kata is a straight line, with movements being made to one side or the other. If the crossed-feet stance is not correct, the performance line tends to bend to the front. So the timing of shifting the body weight and aligning the toes of both feet is important. Whatever the movement, the straddle-leg stance should not be broken. And when exectuing techniques to the side, the body from the hips down mst face forward. Since these kata are rather monotonous, turn the head briskly and strongly.
